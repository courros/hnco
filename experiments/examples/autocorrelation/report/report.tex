\title{HNCO\\
  Visualization of empirical autocorrelation functions\\
  of various functions defined on bit vectors}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
  This document proposes to visualize empirical autocorrelation
  functions of various functions defined on bit vectors (hypercube) of
  size $n=100$. If $f$ is a fitness function, a random walk
  $(X_t)_{t\ge 1}$ on the hypercube gives rise to a time series
  $(f(X_t))$ which is analyzed through its empirical autocorrelation
  function.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The underlying process is a random walk $(X_t)_{t\ge 1}$ on the
hypercube initialized uniformly. If $f$ is the fitness function then
its autocorrelation function $\rho$ is defined by
\begin{equation}
  \rho(\tau) = \frac1{(n - \tau) \sigma^2} \sum_{t=1}^{T-\tau} (f(X_t) -
  \mu)(f(X_{t+\tau}) - \mu)
\end{equation}
where $\mu$ and $\sigma$ are the mean and standard deviation
respectively of the process $(f(X_t))$, $T$ is the length of the
Markov chain and the lag $\tau$ is such that $0 \le \tau < T$. The
empirical autocorrelation function is estimated and computed in a
naive way. It should be noted that the estimated function does not
necessarily have properties such as positivity, monotonicity, or
convexity. Normalized autocorrelation functions $\rho(\tau) / \rho(0)$
are represented in the following sections.
